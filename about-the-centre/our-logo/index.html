MSSRF Logo - Description

The logo design is drawn from the concept of the structure and growth pattern of mangrove vegetation and root system that play a major role in providing some unique ecosystem services. The horizontal vine shown with potential infinite growth on both sides and the hands like structures run out from it stands for the major root system of mangroves. The downwardly pointing two hands like structures represent the branched stilt- roots that grow in harder stands to support the position of adult mangrove trees and the upward hands is representation of the vertical respiratory roots which are crucial for aeration in certain species of mangroves. The circle in which the roots are protruding-in is to evoke mother earth and the hands are to hold on with the Nature. Together with the four compound leaves- the symbolic representation of green vegetation this design symbolises the open ended interactions between human kind and nature and represents the MSSRF vision of functioning like a mangrove ecosystem for the services and benefits needed for sustainable and equitable development in rural areas.

MSSRF Tag Line –Description

The tag line pro- Poor, pro -Nature and pro -Women constitutes the triple bottom line of MSSRF’s work. All our activities are oriented with this dimension and we believe poverty of the people we serve today could be reduced considerably, if there is synergised action in three spheres of interventions  - Science & Technology, Policy Advocacy and Farmer enthusiasm; Similarly, Nature conservation will not become a hard choice for the poor people, if an economic stake is ensured in the product of every conservation action in villages. We also believe women particularly amongst the poor and vulnerable communities will become more visible in decision making both at and outside homes in resource management and livelihood choices, if their economical, social and political capacities are developed through carefully designed project interventions.
